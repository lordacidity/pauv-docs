This page summarizes Pauv's regulatory classification at a high level. Pauv operates consistent with applicable federal and state law, supported by legal opinions from qualified outside counsel.

## Public Sentiment Is Public Domain

Pauv hosts the **Net Public Sentiment Index (NPSI)**: a live, dollar-denominated measure of a public figure's cultural relevance, shaped entirely by forecasting activity. The NPSI is a **derived data product**, similar to a bond rating or a credit score.

How the public perceives a cultural figure is **in the public domain**. It is distinct from the figure's Name, Image, or Likeness, which belongs to that individual. The NPSI quantifies the public's reaction to a figure, not the figure's identity.

Under established precedent (*CBC Distribution and Marketing v. Major League Baseball Advanced Media*, 505 F.3d 818 (8th Cir. 2007)), factual data points about public figures, including measures of cultural trajectory and performance, are in the public domain. Pauv does not "use" any public figure's identity; it indexes the public's reaction to that figure.

<Note>
  Because public sentiment is public domain, no consent is required to list an NPSI. The claim flow exists solely to direct the good-faith royalty, not to grant permission for listing.
</Note>

## Forecasts Are Not Securities

Forecasts opened on an NPSI have been analyzed under the **Howey test** (the standard framework for determining whether an instrument is an "investment contract" under federal securities law) and **do not** constitute securities.

### Key Factors

- **No common enterprise.** Each NPSI is priced by a deterministic, published algorithm (the Dual Curve Model, DTM 2.3). The mathematical rigidity of the bonding curves removes any "common enterprise" element: the index value is a direct measurement of forecasting activity, not a product of centrally managed returns.
- **No managerial efforts.** The NPSI value is driven entirely by user forecasting activity. Pauv does not make managerial decisions that affect the value of any NPSI. Public figures have no role in setting, supporting, or promoting their NPSI value.
- **No ownership or profit participation.** Users do not acquire equity, debt, voting rights, dividend rights, or any interest in a business venture or in any individual.
- **Signal, not asset.** The NPSI is a measure of public perception, not something users "own" or "hold." Users open and close forecasts; they do not hold tradable assets.

### Comparison to Similar Instruments

| Feature | NPSI Forecasts | Prediction-Market Contracts | Securities |
|---------|----------------|------------------------------|------------|
| Binary settlement / event expiry | No | Yes | Varies |
| Confers ownership or profit rights | No | No | Yes |
| Value driven by managerial effort | No | No | Yes |
| Transferable off-platform | No | Varies | Yes |
| Pricing method | Published bonding-curve algorithm | Event-conditional pricing | Market-driven with issuer performance |

Publishing the DTM 2.3 algorithm is a deliberate strategic choice: the mathematical determinism of the pricing mechanism rebuts the "common enterprise" and "efforts of others" prongs of Howey.

## Not a Prediction Market

Unlike prediction markets (e.g., Kalshi, Polymarket) that settle binary outcomes on defined events, the NPSI is a **continuous, non-expiring signal**. There are:

- No binary outcomes
- No event-based settlement
- No expiration dates
- No counterparty-matched positions

Pauv operates a new category called the **signal market**, distinct from both prediction markets and securities markets.

## Not Subject to CFTC Registration

Pauv's activity is limited to forecasts priced by a deterministic bonding curve using internal USD points. The Platform does not offer:

- Futures, options, swaps, or perpetual contracts
- Leveraged, margined, or financed forecasts
- Derivatives of any kind

Therefore, CFTC registration requirements do not apply. The Platform's published pricing algorithm and closed-loop design further mitigate fraud and manipulation risks.

## FinCEN: Money Services Business

Pauv is registered with FinCEN as a **Money Services Business (MSB)** and maintains a full BSA/AML compliance program.

**Points** are classified as **closed-loop stored value**: not a convertible virtual currency (CVC). Points:

- Cannot be transferred between users
- Cannot be sent to external wallets
- Have no extrinsic monetary value
- Function solely as an internal medium for forecasting

Because Pauv's fiat-in / fiat-out model involves receiving and transmitting funds on behalf of users, the Platform operates as a registered money transmitter.

## Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL)

All visual representations of public figures on Pauv are **AI-generated**. Literal photographs (agency-owned headshots or press images) are never used. This is a deliberate compliance choice.

Under the Transformative Use Test, courts have found that synthesized, non-literal depictions are protected "expressive works" rather than commercial appropriations of a person's likeness. Pauv's AI-generated imagery is a stylized representation used to identify an NPSI, itself a public-domain data product, and is not a commercial exploitation of any individual's identity.

## Lanham Act: Non-Endorsement

To prevent any "False Endorsement" claim under the Lanham Act, the Platform's user interface ensures no reasonable consumer would believe a public figure has authorized their NPSI listing:

- Every NPSI profile carries a conspicuous non-endorsement disclaimer.
- Platform copy avoids language like "Buy," "Invest in," or "Official Profile."
- All profiles are titled as NPSI profiles, reinforcing that they are derived data products.

The required disclaimer reads:

> *[Name] is not affiliated with Pauv and has not endorsed, sponsored, or authorized this NPSI listing. All imagery is AI-generated. Forecasts reflect public sentiment, not the views of the person indexed.*

## Good-Faith Royalty (MLC Analogue)

The 0.5% good-faith royalty set aside for public figures mirrors the **Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC)** framework established by the Music Modernization Act. By setting aside royalties in a transparent, audited ledger, Pauv fulfills a duty of care that exceeds the standard for news organizations or fantasy-sports platforms.

Unclaimed royalties are held for the state-mandated dormancy period (typically 3 to 5 years) and then remitted to the applicable State Controller's office under established **escheatment laws**. This structure is supported by precedent in *SEC v. DeSo / BitClout* dispositions, which have recognized the viability of claimable-profile models that use U.S. dollar rails, avoid misappropriation of escrow funds, and maintain transparent public ledgers.

## State Licensing

Pauv's fiat-in / fiat-out model triggers **money-transmitter licensing** in most U.S. states. Pauv is pursuing state-level licensure as required.

At the state level, forecasts on an NPSI are **not considered securities** in any U.S. jurisdiction because they:

- Lack ownership rights, dividend rights, or enterprise-related profit expectations
- Derive value solely from public sentiment as measured by forecasting activity on a published algorithm

## Not a Securities Exchange, Broker-Dealer, or ATS

Because forecasts are not securities, Pauv does not operate as:

- A securities exchange
- A broker or dealer
- An alternative trading system (ATS)

All forecasts are settled against the Dual Curve Model within Pauv's own system. There is no user-to-user matching, no bid/ask spread, and no linkage to national market systems.
