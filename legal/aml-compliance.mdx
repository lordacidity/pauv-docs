Pauv Inc. maintains a comprehensive Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) compliance program designed to prevent the Platform from being used to facilitate money laundering or the financing of terrorist activities.

## Closed-Loop Risk Mitigation

Pauv's closed-loop architecture eliminates the most common avenues for money laundering and fraud by construction:

- **No Peer-to-Peer Transfers.** Users cannot send, gift, or transfer points or internal tokens to other users.
- **No Secondary Markets.** Forecasts are opened and closed only against the Dual Curve Model. There is no order book and no user-to-user matching.
- **No External Portability.** Points and internal tokens are database entries, not blockchain assets. They cannot be transferred off the Platform.
- **Closed-Loop Fiat.** Fiat enters via regulated processors and can only be withdrawn to the same verified bank account the user linked at onboarding.
- **Sequential Processing.** Every forecast is a single API request processed with database-level row locking. Flash loans, sandwich attacks, MEV extraction, and atomic arbitrage are impossible.

## Identity Verification (KYC)

Pauv enforces strict Customer Due Diligence (CDD) through our compliance partner **Soap Payments**, powered by Plaid IDV and Monitor.

### Tier 1: Standard KYC

Required before depositing funds or opening a forecast:

- Data-source verification (name, DOB, address, ID number) against authoritative databases
- SMS verification of your mobile device
- Real-time AML screening against global watchlists

### Tier 2: Step-Up KYC

Required for higher-risk activities. Includes document verification (government-issued photo ID) and selfie liveness check:

- **Claiming an NPSI profile.** Required before a public figure (or their guardian, in the case of a Minor) can direct the good-faith royalty off-platform.
- **Elevated risk signals.** Triggered automatically when the risk engine detects device anomalies, synthetic-identity risk, or low Trust Index scores.
- **Deposit thresholds.** Triggered by unusually large deposits or activity patterns warranting enhanced review.

## OFAC Screening

Pauv is strictly prohibited from conducting business with individuals, entities, or countries sanctioned by the **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)**.

- Every user is automatically screened against the OFAC SDN list and global PEP lists
- Screening is **continuous**: user databases are scrubbed against daily watchlist updates
- If a match is flagged, the account is immediately blocked, balances frozen, and deposit/withdrawal capabilities disabled
- Required blocking reports are filed with the U.S. Treasury/OFAC within 10 business days

## Geofencing

Pauv operates only within the United States. The Platform is **strictly prohibited to international users**:

- All non-US IP addresses are actively blocked
- Account creation is restricted to US-based devices and U.S. bank accounts

## Transaction Monitoring

Pauv monitors for suspicious activity patterns including:

- Structuring deposits just under reporting thresholds
- Large fiat deposits followed by immediate withdrawal requests without forecasting activity
- Rapid deposit-and-withdrawal cycles
- Coordinated round-trip forecasting intended to manipulate an NPSI value
- Fee-farming or wash-forecasting patterns on the Dual Curve Model

Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) are filed with FinCEN when warranted.

## Anti-Manipulation Design

Beyond transaction monitoring, the Dual Curve Model itself is designed to be manipulation-resistant:

- **Flat 1.8% fee.** Every forecast, on every leg, pays the same gross-cash fee. This turns theoretical path-dependence attacks on exponential bonding curves into a money-losing proposition.
- **Early-exit guardrail.** A 5.0% fee on forecasts closed within 10 minutes (FIFO) deters rapid round-trip manipulation while leaving genuine forecasters unaffected.
- **No counterparty.** Because every forecast settles against a published algorithm, there are no hidden order flows to exploit.
- **Single-attacker profile.** Centralized Web2 architecture and sequential processing mean the only viable attacker is a real person using their own funds, one request at a time.

## MSB Registration

Pauv Inc. is a registered **Money Services Business (MSB)** with FinCEN, maintaining a full BSA/AML compliance program including:

- Customer Identification Program (CIP) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for higher-risk users
- OFAC and sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring and SAR/CTR procedures
- Employee training on AML/BSA requirements
- Independent testing of AML program and internal controls
