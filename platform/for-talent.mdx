## Why Your NPSI Exists

Every public figure on Pauv has a **Net Public Sentiment Index (NPSI)**: a live, dollar-denominated measure of their cultural relevance. The NPSI exists because **public sentiment is public domain**. How the world perceives a cultural figure is not owned by that figure, unlike their Name, Image, or Likeness.

Pauv does not ask public figures to "agree to be listed." Listing an NPSI is not a commercial use of identity; it is indexing the public's reaction to it. The NPSI is a derived data product, similar to a bond rating or a credit score.

<Note>
  All visual representations of public figures on Pauv are **AI-generated**. No literal photographs, agency headshots, or press images are ever used. Every profile carries a clear non-endorsement disclaimer stating the figure is not affiliated with or endorsed by Pauv.
</Note>

## The Good-Faith Royalty

Every forecast on Pauv carries a **0.5% good-faith royalty** that is set aside for the public figure whose NPSI is being forecasted. This is a good-faith gesture, designed to demonstrate that Pauv acts in the interest of the people it indexes.

<Warning>
  The good-faith royalty is **not** a salary, dividend, passive income stream, revenue share, or payment for services. It is a set-aside that the public figure may claim, donate, or leave unclaimed.
</Warning>

| Set-aside | Rate | Source |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Good-faith royalty | 0.5% of every forecast | Deducted as part of the total 1.8% fee |

Royalties accumulate in a transparent, audited ledger against each NPSI profile, regardless of whether the public figure has ever interacted with Pauv.

## Claim Your Profile

Every NPSI profile features a prominent **"Claim Your Profile"** button. When a public figure claims their profile, they complete identity verification and choose what happens to their accrued and future royalties:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Accept" icon="wallet">
    Direct accrued and future royalties to your verified bank account as USD deposits.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Donate" icon="heart">
    Direct all accrued and future royalties to a verified 501(c)(3) charity. The chosen charity is publicly displayed on your NPSI profile.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

If no one claims the profile, funds are held for the state-mandated dormancy period (typically 3 to 5 years) and then remitted to the applicable State Controller's office as unclaimed property under established escheatment laws.

## What Claiming Is: and Is Not

Claiming your profile **only** determines where the good-faith royalty is directed. It is not an endorsement, partnership, or authorization.

- Claiming **does not** constitute endorsement of, affiliation with, or authorization of Pauv.
- Claiming **does not** grant Pauv any rights to your Name, Image, or Likeness.
- Claiming **does not** create a partnership, sponsorship, or business relationship.
- Claiming **does not** give you control over the NPSI value, forecasting behavior, or the presentation of your profile.

The NPSI is driven entirely by forecasting activity on the platform. Public figures have no role in setting, managing, or supporting the value.

## How to Claim

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your profile">
    Visit your NPSI profile on pauv.com and select **Claim Your Profile**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete identity verification">
    Pauv uses standard KYC plus Step-Up verification (government-issued photo ID and selfie liveness check) to confirm you are the individual represented by the profile. This is required before any funds can leave the platform.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Accept or Donate">
    Direct your accrued and future royalties to your verified bank account, or designate a verified 501(c)(3) to receive them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monthly disbursement">
    Accrued royalties are disbursed to the destination you selected each month, paid in USD fiat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Minor Public Figures

Individuals under 18 whose cultural relevance merits an NPSI may have profiles on Pauv. For any royalty to be directed off-platform, a parent or legal guardian must complete identity verification and manage the claim on the Minor's behalf. The guardian may review the Minor's profile, designate a charity, or request that future royalties be held or escheated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to sign anything for my NPSI to exist?">
    No. NPSI profiles exist because public sentiment is public domain. No consent is required for a public figure to be indexed, and no one at Pauv "signs" or "partners with" any individual to list their NPSI.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I take my NPSI down?">
    The NPSI itself is a derived data product reflecting public sentiment and is not subject to removal on request. However, you can at any time use the claim flow to donate, redirect, or refuse the good-faith royalty set aside for you.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my image on Pauv look like a photo of me?">
    No. All imagery on Pauv is AI-generated. Pauv does not use agency-owned headshots, press photos, or any literal photographic likeness.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does claiming make me a Pauv partner or endorser?">
    No. Claiming directs where the good-faith royalty is paid. Every NPSI profile explicitly states that the public figure is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Pauv.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I don't want the money?">
    You can donate it. Every claim flow lets you direct all accrued and future royalties to a verified 501(c)(3) charity, which is then publicly displayed on your profile. If you never claim, funds are eventually escheated to the state as unclaimed property.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
