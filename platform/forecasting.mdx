## What Is a Forecast?

A **forecast** is a directional view on a public figure's cultural relevance, expressed through their NPSI. You are not buying, selling, or owning anything — you are opening a position against Pauv's Dual Curve Model that moves the live index value up or down.

| Forecast Type | What It Means |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Positive Forecast** | You believe the figure's cultural relevance will rise. Pulls the NPSI up. |
| **Negative Forecast** | You believe the figure's cultural relevance will fall. Pulls the NPSI down. |

<Warning>
  Forecasts are not securities, ownership interests, investment contracts, or wagers on a binary event. They are continuous, directional signals priced by the Dual Curve Model for entertainment and engagement purposes only.
</Warning>

## Opening a Forecast

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a public figure">
    Browse NPSI profiles on Pauv. Every public figure on the platform has an NPSI — a live, dollar-denominated measure of their cultural relevance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a direction">
    Decide whether you think the NPSI is undervalued (open a Positive Forecast) or overvalued (open a Negative Forecast).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Commit credits">
    Enter the amount of credits (1 credit = $1 USD) you want to commit. The 1.8% fee is deducted first, and the remaining 98.2% enters the appropriate curve.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settlement is instant">
    The Dual Curve Model settles your forecast immediately. There is no order book, no counterparty, and no waiting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Closing a Forecast

When you are ready to exit, close your forecast. The internal tokens representing your forecast are returned to the curve, and the curve calculates your gross proceeds. The 1.8% fee is deducted, and you receive the net payout in credits.

If you open a forecast and immediately close it with no other activity, you receive exactly your original amount in gross proceeds — minus the fees on each leg.

| Platform fee | 1.3% | Pauv |
| Good-faith royalty | 0.5% | Set aside for the public figure |

## Why Both Directions Matter

Prior platforms that tried to quantify attention on people — BitClout and Friend.tech among them — offered only one direction. That made their indices one-sided and manipulable. Pauv's Dual Curve Model supports both Positive and Negative Forecasts, producing a more accurate, self-correcting signal. When the NPSI drifts away from what the market believes is accurate, there is an incentive to push it back in either direction.

## Important Rules

- **No peer-to-peer transfers.** Credits and internal tokens cannot be sent to other users or external wallets.
- **No off-platform trading.** Internal tokens are database records, not blockchain assets. They cannot be moved off Pauv.
- **No shorting in the traditional sense.** A Negative Forecast is a directional signal on the negative curve, not a borrowed-asset short position.
- **No leverage.** You can only commit credits you have deposited. There is no margin and no lending.
- **Volatility.** The NPSI can move sharply. A Negative Forecast does not protect you from Positive Forecast momentum on the same figure.
