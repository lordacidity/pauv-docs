## The Net Public Sentiment Index

Every public figure on Pauv has an **NPSI**: a live, dollar-denominated measure of their cultural relevance. The value is determined solely by forecasting activity on Pauv. There are no social metrics, no algorithmic feeds, and no external data sources pushed into the index.

- **Floor at zero.** No matter how much negative forecasting happens, the NPSI approaches zero asymptotically but never reaches it.
- **No ceiling.** The NPSI can grow without bound.
- **Not an asset.** Users do not own the NPSI. It is a measure of public perception, not a financial instrument.

When a figure drops a hit, wins a title, or goes viral, users react, forecasts get opened, and the NPSI moves in real time.

## Positive and Negative Forecasts

Users interact with Pauv by opening and closing forecasts. A forecast is a directional view on a public figure's cultural relevance, expressed through the NPSI.

| Forecast Type | What It Means | Effect on NPSI |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Positive Forecast** | The user believes the figure's cultural relevance will rise. | Pulls the NPSI value **up**. |
| **Negative Forecast** | The user believes the figure's cultural relevance will fall. | Pulls the NPSI value **down**. |

## The Single Curve Model (DTM 4.0)

**DTM 4.0** is Pauv's proprietary pricing engine. Every NPSI is governed by a single continuous bonding curve. There are no separate pools, no order books, no liquidity providers, and no counterparty matching.

The curve has one state variable, **Q**, representing net token supply for that NPSI. Positive Forecasts push Q up; Negative Forecasts push Q down. Both directions use the same published price function, with a softplus floor that guarantees the price stays strictly above zero.

### How Opening and Closing Affects the NPSI

| Action | Effect on the Curve | NPSI |
|--------|---------------------|------|
| Open Positive Forecast | Q moves up | Rises |
| Close Positive Forecast | Q moves down | Falls |
| Open Negative Forecast | Q moves down | Falls |
| Close Negative Forecast | Q moves up | Rises |

### Positive Forecasts (Longs)

Opening a Positive Forecast commits points to the curve, raising Q. The user receives an internal token position sized by the curve's integral between the old and new Q. Closing the position reverses the integral back down the curve and returns the proceeds as points — a gain if the NPSI rose while the forecast was open, a loss if it fell.

### Negative Forecasts (Shorts)

Opening a Negative Forecast pulls Q down using **escrow-based shorting**: the user's net stake is matched dollar-for-dollar by capital extracted from the curve, and both amounts are held in an **escrow** equal to twice the net stake. No tokens are borrowed from other users, no margin is extended, and no additional capital can ever be required from the user. Closing the forecast uses the escrow to buy the tokens back along the curve; whatever remains after the buy-back is returned to the user as their net proceeds.

- If the NPSI fell while the forecast was open, the buy-back costs less than the net stake and the user receives more than they committed.
- If the NPSI rose, the buy-back costs more than the net stake and the user receives less — possibly zero.

<Warning>
  **Liquidation.** A Negative Forecast is automatically closed if its buy-back cost reaches **95% of its escrow**. At that point the full escrow is consumed buying the tokens back, and the user's net return is zero. This is the hard loss bound of a Negative Forecast: you cannot lose more than your net stake, but you can lose all of it in a sharp adverse move of the NPSI.
</Warning>

### Initial Pricing: Elo-Based Comparative Pricing

When a new NPSI profile is created, its starting value is set through **Elo-Based Comparative Pricing**. The new profile is shown alongside two existing profiles, and participants rank where they think the new one should land: above, between, or below. Iterating thousands of times with random combinations converges on an accurate starting value.

If the starting value is slightly off at launch, the market self-corrects: forecasting activity pushes the NPSI to where the market believes it belongs.

## Fees

Every forecast, whether opening or closing, incurs a flat **1.8%** fee on the gross cash amount.

| Component | Rate | Recipient |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Platform fee | 1.3% | Pauv |
| Good-faith royalty | 0.5% | Set aside for the public figure |
| **Total** | **1.8%** | **Split** |

- **Opening a forecast**: The 1.8% fee is deducted from the committed amount; the remaining 98.2% enters the curve as the user's net stake.
- **Closing a forecast**: The curve calculates gross proceeds; the 1.8% fee is deducted from those proceeds, and the user receives the net payout.
- **Liquidation**: If a Negative Forecast is auto-closed at the liquidation threshold, any remaining escrow after the buy-back would be reduced by the 1.8% fee before being returned. At the 95% threshold, the remaining return is zero in practice.

A round trip in a quiet index (open then close with no other activity) costs approximately **3.57%**: the compounded effect of paying 1.8% on both legs.

## Closed-Loop Architecture

Pauv operates as a fully closed system. This is a deliberate design choice that eliminates entire categories of manipulation by construction.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="No peer-to-peer transfers" icon="ban">
    Points and internal tokens cannot be sent to other users, wallets, or platforms.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No secondary markets" icon="shield-halved">
    Forecasts are opened and closed only against DTM 4.0.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No blockchain dependency" icon="lock">
    Internal tokens are database records, not on-chain assets. They cannot leave Pauv.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No leverage or borrowing" icon="building-columns">
    Users can only commit points they have deposited. Negative Forecasts are escrow-backed, not borrowed. No margin, no lending.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

This architecture makes flash loans, sandwich attacks, MEV extraction, and atomic arbitrage impossible. Every forecast is a single request processed sequentially via database-level row locking.

## Sentiment Score

Alongside the headline NPSI value, Pauv exposes a **sentiment score**: the net signed dollars committed to the curve. Positive Forecasts contribute positively, Negative Forecasts contribute negatively, and the score is the integral of price over the curve's range. The sentiment score measures capital at stake, while the NPSI value measures how the index is priced.

<Note>
  Forecasts on the NPSI are not securities, ownership interests, or investment contracts. They are opened and closed against a published algorithm for entertainment and signal purposes only.
</Note>
